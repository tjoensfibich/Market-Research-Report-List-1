<p><p>Chromogenic culture media is designed to differentiate and identify microorganisms based on color changes. Natural media provides organic nutrients, supporting a wide range of bacteria but may lack specificity. Mixed media combines natural substrates with selective agents to target specific pathogens while inhibiting others. Semi-composite media blends elements of both, offering versatility for various organisms by enabling growth and differentiation. Each type plays a crucial role in microbiological analysis, enhancing the detection and identification of pathogens in clinical and environmental settings.</p></p>
