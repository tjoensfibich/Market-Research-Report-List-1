<p><p>Cheese making cultures can be categorized into three primary types: Mesophilic, Thermophilic, and Probiotic. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, typically used in soft and semi-soft cheese production, providing desirable flavors and textures. Thermophilic cultures operate at higher temperatures, essential for hard cheeses and those requiring prolonged cooking. Probiotics are beneficial cultures added for health benefits, enhancing gut health while contributing to cheese's flavor and texture. Each type plays a crucial role in the diverse cheese market.</p></p>
