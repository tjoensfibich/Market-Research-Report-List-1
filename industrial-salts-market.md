<p><p>The industrial salts market primarily consists of two types: brine and salt mined from deposits. Brine, a concentrated saltwater solution, is extracted through evaporation processes and is often used in chemical production and de-icing. Salt mines, on the other hand, involve mining solid rock salt from underground deposits, providing high-purity sodium chloride for various applications, including food preservation and de-icing roads. Both sources play crucial roles in industries such as chemicals, food, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
